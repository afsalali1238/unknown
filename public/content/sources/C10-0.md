# Nikola founder Trevor Milton sentencing: fraud charges

Nikola founder Trevor Milton was found guilty in October 2022 on two counts of wire fraud and one count of securities fraud, facing up to 60 years in prison (prosecutors recommended 11 years). Milton became a billionaire overnight when he took electric- and hydrogen-truck maker Nikola public via a SPAC merger in June 2020, at one point valuing the company at more than $30 billion.

Short-seller Hindenburg Research uncovered the allegations of false and misleading statements that unraveled the company — most famously that a promotional video showing a Nikola truck "driving" was actually the vehicle rolling downhill. Milton stepped down as executive chairman in September 2020 following an internal probe after Hindenburg's report, which characterized Nikola as a house of cards built on Milton's claims.

Prosecutors compared Milton to Theranos founder Elizabeth Holmes, sentenced to over 11 years for defrauding investors in her blood-testing startup: "Just as Holmes lied about Theranos-manufactured blood analyzers, Milton lied about the operability of the Nikola One semitruck." Milton argued Nikola, unlike Theranos, was "still a real business." Nikola shares, which never recovered, were trading under $1 with a market value of about $327 million at the time of this report — down from a peak valuation more than 90 times higher.
