# One year after Hindenburg's Adani report, the tycoon has bounced back

In January 2023, U.S. short-seller Hindenburg Research published a report accusing Gautam Adani — then Asia's richest man — of "brazen stock manipulation" and decades of fraud across his ports-to-power conglomerate, questioning the "sky-high" valuations of Adani Group firms and flagging their "substantial debt." Hindenburg disclosed it held a short position, meaning it would profit if Adani's shares fell.

Adani Group called the report "baseless" and "malicious," publishing a rebuttal running more than 400 pages and accusing Hindenburg's own shorting of overseas bonds and derivatives of amounting to securities fraud. Regardless, the report triggered a stock market meltdown that wiped over $100 billion off the value of Adani's listed companies at its worst point, and more than $80 billion off Adani's personal fortune within a month.

A year later, Adani had largely recovered: his wealth crossed $100 billion again, shares in most of his 10 listed firms rallied to record highs, group net debt fell 3.5% to $21.73 billion, and the conglomerate attracted nearly $5 billion in new foreign investment, including from U.S. private equity firm GQG Partners. India's top court also ordered market regulator SEBI to wrap up its investigation, finding no grounds for further probes. Critics noted the comeback rested partly on Adani's close relationship with Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi, but by the time of this report Adani ranked as the world's 12th-richest person.
