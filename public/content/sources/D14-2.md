Call held July 15, 2026, covering Q2 2026 results. ASML (sole global supplier of EUV lithography machines, required to manufacture the most advanced logic and memory chips) reported Q2 net sales of €9.3 billion, exceeding the high end of guidance, with gross margin of 54% — above expectations due to high-margin components in the install-base management business. Net income was €2.9 billion (31.3% of net sales); EPS €7.59. Cash and short-term investments totaled €7.6 billion; free cash flow €1.3 billion.

ASML raised full-year 2026 guidance to €43–45 billion net sales, 54–56% gross margin, driven by "strong customer demand driven by AI accelerator needs and DRAM price increases," per the earnings summary. The company expects 25% growth in advanced-logic-foundry-related net system sales and 75% growth in memory-related net system sales for 2026. It plans to ship roughly 65 low-NA EUV systems and 130 immersion DUV systems in 2026, with EUV net system sales growing over 45%.

CFO Roger Dassen, on capacity: "We are now close to being fully covered with orders for low NA EUV, and we are planning to increase our low NA EUV capacity by around 30%... Looking ahead to 2028, we have already received a significant number of low NA EUV orders. Strong demand forecasts from our customers have led us to investigate a further 30% capacity increase for that year." Immersion system capacity is also planned to grow 30% in 2027 with a further potential 30% expansion investigated for 2028.

When an analyst (Krish Sankar, TD Cowen) asked whether ASML was fully meeting demand or under-shipping, Dassen replied: "the capacity is there to meet the demand but the demand is still fluctuating. We get nicer visibility for sure for 27, even 28. But we also mostly are not at the end of the discussion with our customer." When pressed on why the company is "investigating" a further 30% capacity increase for 2028 rather than committing outright, Dassen said: "We wouldn't investigate 110 if customers were telling us that [demand] would be a ridiculous number. So we're investigating this simply because customers are signaling to us that the demand is very, very strong."
