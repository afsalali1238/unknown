Posted July 13, 2026: "How to break the memory supercycle? Or is it even possible? A few days ago, SK Group Chairman said that customers are asking for 4x-5x more supply, but wafer supply will only double by 2x by 2030. So, I got quite curious about this, and I want to know if it [is even possible to close the gap]." (Full thread continues via a linked X Article; the tweet itself links out rather than containing the complete analysis.)

**Independent corroboration (Tom's Hardware, reporting on Bloomberg, June 2, 2026):** SK hynix chairman Chey Tae-won told reporters at Computex in Taipei that SK hynix will double its memory wafer capacity within five years, while reiterating his forecast that the AI-driven memory shortage will persist until at least 2030. Chey put the lead time for a new ("greenfield") fab at more than five years — meaning fresh output lands near the tail end of the shortage window he is forecasting, not before it. This was a reversal from his March 2026 comments at Nvidia's GTC conference, where he'd said a new fab wasn't planned and that capacity couldn't be added on demand. With existing lines already saturated, customers have reportedly offered to buy SK hynix's EUV scanners outright and prefund new fab lines, as available capacity has fallen to near zero.

The underlying mechanism cited: HBM (high-bandwidth memory) consumes far more silicon wafers per bit than standard DRAM and carries the industry's highest margins, so new capacity keeps tilting toward HBM production — which further starves standard DRAM supply. SK hynix holds roughly 57% of the global HBM market and 32% of global DRAM. TrendForce (cited in the same report) projected DRAM contract prices to rise 63% quarter-over-quarter after already climbing roughly 95% in the prior quarter; DDR4 spot pricing rose approximately 2,200% over the preceding twelve months before a partial recent pullback.
