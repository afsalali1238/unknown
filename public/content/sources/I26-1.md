Published July 15, 2026. Inkling, released by Thinking Machines, is a large (~1T parameters, 975B total / 41B active, Mixture-of-Experts) open multimodal model natively accepting image, text, and audio input, with a 1M-token context window, trained on 45 trillion tokens of text, image, audio, and video. It ships in full BF16 and a calibrated NVFP4 variant, with day-0 support in transformers, SGLang, vLLM, and llama.cpp.

On the published benchmark table, Inkling scores: HLE (text only) 29.7%, HLE (with tools) 46.0%, AIME 2026 97.1%, GPQA Diamond 87.2%, SWEBench Verified 77.6%, SWEBench Pro (Public) 54.3%, MCP Atlas 74.1%, BrowseComp (w/ Ctx) 77.1%, SimpleQA Verified 43.9%, MMMU Pro (Standard 10) 73.3%. For comparison, on the same table: Claude Fable 5 (max) scores 53.3% on HLE (text only) and 95.0% on SWEBench Verified; GPT 5.6 Sol (xhigh) scores 47.2% on HLE (text only) and 89.4% on BrowseComp; GLM 5.2 scores 40.1% on HLE (text only) and 82.7% on Terminal Bench 2.1.

Notable on the "AA Omniscience" factuality metric — designed to penalize confident wrong answers — Inkling scores 1.0%, Nemotron 3 Ultra scores -1.0%, Kimi K2.5 scores -8.0%, DeepSeek V4 Pro scores -10.0%, while Gemini 3.1 Pro (high) scores 33.0%, Claude Fable 5 (max) scores 40.0%, and GPT 5.6 Sol (xhigh) scores 22.0% — illustrating a wide spread in calibration/hallucination-resistance even among frontier-adjacent models on the same evaluation.

The post's own framing: Inkling is "great for building a new wave of multimodal reasoning apps" and is intended primarily for fine-tuning/domain adaptation rather than as a standalone frontier chat model — its scores trail the largest closed frontier models (Claude Fable 5, GPT-5.6 Sol, Gemini 3.1 Pro) across most reasoning, agentic, and factuality categories in the same table.
