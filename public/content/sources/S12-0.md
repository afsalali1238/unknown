Dan's lifelong desire: to become "multidimensionally jacked" — maxed out physicality, intellect, and professions, not an NPC. Over 15 years he's researched psychology, personal development, philosophy, social dynamics, technology, startups, money, religion, and meaning, and Human 3.0 is his attempt to synthesize it into one map — non-dogmatic, science-backed where possible, explicitly open to being wrong and built on by readers.

Existing models (Spiral Dynamics, Buddhism, Christianity, Materialism/Mentalism, Red Pill/Feminism, eCommerce/consulting) each hold truths but are siloed to one domain — many spiritual teachers have frail bodies, many businessmen can't maintain relationships. Human 3.0 tries to cover mind, body, spirit, _and_ vocation/money together, which older philosophies (pre-internet/AI) rarely did.

## 1) Quadrants

Adapted from Ken Wilber's AQAL model — four domains of life:

- **Mind** (Personal Mental World) — thoughts, emotions, beliefs, internal world.
- **Body** (Personal Physical World) — behavior and physical appearance; nutrition, training, habits, apparel.
- **Spirit** (Collective Mental World) — relationship to community, culture, family, meaning, connection.
- **Vocation** (Collective Physical World) — relationship to systems, institutions, education, career, economy.

Money often doesn't solve for meaning and vice versa, but the domains are intimately connected. Life/development is framed as a general process of "problem solving": desire to reach potential → step into the unknown/complexity → acquire knowledge and skill to solve the problem → identity expands to a new level → repeat.

## 2) Levels

Within each quadrant, three macro levels (adapted from Spiral Dynamics / stages of ego development):

- **Human 1.0 (The Conformist)** — values established authority and tradition, black-and-white thinking, "one right way," often from childhood conditioning.
- **Human 2.0 (The Individualist)** — rejects the norm, pursues own goals and status, still believes _their_ way is the one right way.
- **Human 3.0 (The Synthesist)** — adopts multiple perspectives, connects patterns, synthesizes truths from all levels; can display level-1-looking traits as an intentional choice to tune out noise.

You don't leave a level — you transcend and include it, keeping the knowledge/skill/tools acquired at lower stages.

## 3) Phases

Within each level, three phases of vertical development:

- **Dissonance** — tired of where you are, unsure what's next.
- **Uncertainty** — aware enough of the distaste to take an uncertain step into the unknown.
- **Discovery** — finding the education, tools, and insight to reach the next level.

Notation example: "Vocation 2.1" = Individualist level, phase 1 (nearly exhausted that stage). Watch for **False Transformation** — imitating what the next level looks like without the underlying trait development (e.g. advancing in Mind and wrongly assuming Spirit or Vocation advanced too). Regression to a lower level can happen under stress.

## 4) Traits

Horizontal development within a phase — the threshold of knowledge/experience/skill needed before the next phase reveals itself. Knowledge without practice produces the "fat personal trainer" archetype. Jumping levels without the skill/experience causes anxiety and failure; refusing to move at all causes boredom — both are disorder.

## 5) Channels

Reaching the Dissonance phase unlocks the ability to enter a "Channel" — an absorbing rabbit hole of knowledge/skill (deep meditation or an idea that keeps you up at night; obsessive training; a philosophy that "clicks"; a career pivot you can't stop learning for). Entering one: reach Dissonance → set an aim within a quadrant → acquire knowledge and skill → refine the aim through mistakes → find the Channel.

**"Glitches"** are tactics to force a Channel or bust a plateau — psychedelics, PEDs, an unaffordable apartment that forces a deadline, and now AI (the most widely available glitch, crossing all domains). These accelerate progress but increase risk at lower levels of consciousness; without a base of knowledge and experience, "the AI will have nasty side effects" (AI psychosis, outsourcing your mind to AI).

## Archetypes & Metatypes

**Archetypes** are patterns of people in a specific quadrant/level (e.g. Mind: NPC → Player → Creator; Vocation: Job → Career → Calling; Spirit: Religion → Atheism → Mysticism). **Metatypes** are the synthesis of a person's four archetypes across all quadrants — like a self-development personality profile, scored by weighting Level 1/2/3 points and averaging across 12 developmental slices to get a number like "Human 2.0."

Ken Wilber's _pre-trans fallacy_ is referenced: people confuse pre-rational (Conformist) states with trans-rational (Synthesist) states because both look "non-rational" from a conventional (Individualist) view.

## The 3.0 Lifestyle

The goal is a lifestyle where all four quadrants are accounted for through ongoing problem-solving — if Vocation consumes all your time, Mind and Body suffer and Spirit goes untended; solving that may require development _outside_ the domain that seems broken (e.g. a business plateau solved by fixing energy/health rather than more business skill). Work becomes play, health becomes default, meaning becomes abundant.
