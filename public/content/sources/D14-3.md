Published June 16, 2026, as a video/transcript piece featuring Kristina Nilsson (Global Co-Head of AI Investment Banking) and Justin Krauss (Security and Resiliency Initiative lead for Defense Tech and Innovation Economy). Central framing, from Nilsson: "Think about AI almost like an enabling liquid for human beings to function in the future. And that enabling liquid has a lot of physical infrastructure behind it... someone said recently, 'Think about the magic that's happening here. You're taking sand and transforming it into thought.'"

Krauss, on the data center build-out: "The question is, all those pieces down the line, do you have the workers to actually build the data center? Can you get the chips, which is a big question. Can you get the turbines you might need to produce the power that comes onto the grid." He frames autonomy (drones, autonomous vehicles, logistics) as a parallel accelerating trend: "however many autonomous things you see today, tomorrow you'll see more and the next day you'll see more."

Cited figures: global software spending, driven by digital transformation and generative AI integration, is projected to reach around $2 trillion by 2029; 40% of applications are projected to include agentic AI by 2029, up from less than 1% in 2024. J.P. Morgan's Security and Resiliency Initiative is described as a $1.5 trillion, 10-year plan to finance and invest in industries deemed critical to economic security and resiliency — spanning cybersecurity, AI, quantum computing, and sensor hardware, alongside supply chain/manufacturing, defense/aerospace, and energy independence.
