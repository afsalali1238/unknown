Published July 17, 2026. TrendForce frames the humanoid robot industry as moving from technology validation to commercial trials, with competition shifting to "who can build a sustainable business model and who controls key components."

**US strategy — intelligence over hardware.** The report's core claim: "the value of humanoid robots is shifting from hardware specifications to intelligence." NVIDIA continues advancing Physical AI platforms (Cosmos, Isaac Lab, GR00T); Google DeepMind has launched Gemini Robotics; OpenAI participates via investment and research. US firms — Tesla (Optimus Gen 3), Figure AI, Boston Dynamics, Apptronik — are "focused on building a data flywheel, aiming to continuously refine their models with large volumes of real-world data and differentiate their products through AI capability," rather than primarily competing on hardware specs.

**China strategy — supply chain scale over iteration speed.** TrendForce compares China's approach explicitly to "the early development model of electric vehicles: building a complete supply chain and lowering costs quickly, then achieving economies of scale through high shipment volumes." Representative firms: Unitree (reputation for value-for-money and rapid iteration) and AgiBot, which scaled production from 1,000 to 5,000 units in about a year, then from 5,000 to 10,000 units in three more months. TrendForce's assessment: "China's advantage today extends beyond manufacturing cost. Its supply chain maturity allows new products to complete component validation, pilot runs, and mass production quickly." The report also describes a possible feedback loop: "deploy, collect data, update the model, redeploy" — meaning China's manufacturing scale could translate into a data-volume advantage that narrows the AI capability gap over time.

**Supply chain control (CSCII index).** TrendForce's proprietary Component Supply Chain Influence Index rates regions across four component "planes": Sensing, Mental (AI chips/compute), Movement (motors, actuators), and Power. By their rating: China has the most comprehensive presence across all four planes and leads the Power plane; the US holds the most pronounced leadership in the Mental plane via NVIDIA and Qualcomm's near-monopoly on AI chips and compute platforms; Europe is rated "Strong" in Sensing and Movement (precision manufacturing, e.g. German encoder makers); Japan leads in Movement via mechanical transmission expertise; South Korea is rated "Strong" in Power via LG and Samsung battery investment.

TrendForce's own conclusion: "China's rapid rise is putting real pressure on US manufacturers today. But over the long run, what will determine the global humanoid robot industry's landscape is whether companies can build business models that combine scale deployment, data accumulation, and sustainable profitability... TrendForce believes that the future market is unlikely to be winner-take-all — multiple players are likely to coexist."
