# Carson Block, the man who felled a forestry giant

Carson Block, founder of Muddy Waters LLC, published a June 2011 research report alleging Sino-Forest Corp — then the largest Canadian-listed forestry company — was a "fraud" and a "multibillion-dollar Ponzi scheme," wiping out roughly $3 billion in market value. Sino-Forest CEO Allen Chan called Block a self-interested "shock jock"; Block responded: "You know what industry I do understand pretty well? The fraud industry. So I don't need to know trees to know fraud."

Muddy Waters — named for the Chinese proverb "muddy waters make it easier to catch fish" — built its case using Block's own business experience in China (he had run a struggling self-storage venture in Shanghai) and unconventional tactics, including misrepresenting himself in face-to-face meetings with company representatives to test their claims. He described disguising himself for one meeting with Sino-Forest's head of investor relations: "I even combed my hair forward for a 'Dumb and Dumber' kind of look... I just tried to seem like an idiot."

Notably, all seven Wall Street analysts covering Sino-Forest rated the stock a "buy" before Block's report. Sino-Forest's share price fell from more than $20 to below $5, and the company initiated an independent PricewaterhouseCoopers investigation. A Globe and Mail on-the-ground investigation in China separately found discrepancies between Sino-Forest's public statements and what Chinese forestry officials and a key company partner in Yunnan Province described.

On his role: "I didn't wipe it out. Management that commits fraud wipes it out... I protected them against losing more money and that is what I feel good about."
