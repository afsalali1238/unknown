Renaissance Technologies LLC (also known as RenTec or RenTech) is an American hedge fund based in East Setauket, New York, that specializes in systematic trading using quantitative models derived from mathematical and statistical analysis. Renaissance was founded in 1982 by James Simons, a mathematician who worked as a code breaker during the Cold War.

In 1988, the firm established the Medallion Fund, using an expanded form of Leonard Baum's mathematical models, improved by algebraist James Ax, to explore correlations from which it could profit. The fund was named Medallion in honor of the math awards Simons and Ax had won.

The firm uses quantitative trading, tapping a petabyte-scale data warehouse to assess statistical probabilities for security price direction. Renaissance employs specialists with non-financial backgrounds — computer scientists, mathematicians, physicists, signal-processing experts, and statisticians — roughly 150 researchers and programmers, about half with PhDs in scientific disciplines.

By April 1989, peak-to-trough losses had mounted to about 30%. Simons paused and reevaluated; Ax departed. Simons brought in Berkeley professor Elwyn Berlekamp, who overhauled Medallion's trading system over six months. In 1990, Berlekamp led Medallion to a 55.9% gain net of fees, then returned to Berkeley. Sandor Straus took over, and the revamped system returned 39.4% in 1991, 34% in 1992, and 39.1% in 1993.

The Medallion fund is considered one of the most successful hedge funds ever. From 1994 through mid-2014, it averaged a 71.8% annual return before fees. It has been closed to outside investors since 1993 and is available only to current and past employees and their families.

"Since 1988, his flagship Medallion fund has generated average annual returns of 66% before charging hefty investor fees — 39% after fees — racking up trading gains of more than $100 billion. No one in the investment world comes close. Warren Buffett, George Soros, Peter Lynch, Steve Cohen, and Ray Dalio all fall short." — Gregory Zuckerman, "The Man Who Solved the Market: How Jim Simons Launched the Quant Revolution" (2019)

Between January 1993 and April 2005, Medallion had only 17 losing months out of that period, and out of 49 quarters, only three losing quarters. Between 1989 and 2005, Medallion had only one losing year: 1989. During 2020, the Medallion fund surged 76%. From 2001 through 2013, the fund's worst year was still a 21% gain after fees; it reaped a 98.2% gain in 2008, the year the S&P 500 lost 38.5%.

Renaissance is run by and for scientists — Wall Street experience is frowned upon and a flair for science is prized, according to the New York Times.
