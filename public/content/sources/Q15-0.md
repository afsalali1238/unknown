# 33 Powerful Phrases That Show Active Listening

Drawing on several communication experts and therapists, this piece collects concrete phrases and body-language cues that demonstrate genuine active listening, as opposed to passively waiting for a turn to speak.

Foundational moves: give focused attention and remove distractions — glancing at a phone or the TV signals "you're not that important" even if unintentional. Lean in and maintain eye contact where culturally appropriate, both of which signal investment in what the other person is saying. Beyond body language, specific phrases do real work: "please tell me more" and "go on" invite the speaker to continue rather than assuming the conversation has ended; paraphrasing without parroting ("is that correct?" or "do I have that right?") confirms understanding was genuinely absorbed, not just heard; and naming the other person's emotional state back to them ("it can be so frustrating when people we care about misinterpret us") signals empathy and builds trust.

For deeper engagement, direct follow-up questions outperform generic encouragement: "what led you to that conclusion?", "could you clarify?", and "is there another related issue regarding that?" all push a conversation toward genuine understanding rather than surface-level small talk. The piece also flags what active listening is not: repetitive filler like "very good" or "yes" used too casually reads as inattentive rather than engaged, and judgmental framings ("why would you do that?") shut a speaker down — reframing as "what motivated you to make that choice?" keeps the door open. The consistent throughline across all 33 phrases: active listening is demonstrated through specific, effortful responses to what was actually said, not generic verbal filler.
