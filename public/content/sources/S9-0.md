Unfortunately, all jobs will be gone in like the next 5 seconds. At least that's what it feels like if you take loud people on social media a bit too seriously - like my article title.

AI isn't the threat you think it is. The real threat is what it's always been: you are dependent on everyone but yourself for your survival and well-being. Technology of any form is always going to threaten that.

I have 4 ideas to share with you on wage slavery, becoming high agency, and why all of these ideas don't mean a thing unless you fundamentally change who you are.

## I – How to escape wage slavery

Wage slavery is doing meaningless grind work you didn't choose, for someone else, just to survive. I am not anti-job — jobs are worthy stepping stones for practical experience and skill acquisition. But enjoyment, meaning, and fulfillment come from living at the edge of your abilities (flow). The thing about jobs is that after a few months, you know everything you need to know. You get bored. It's against your nature.

Civilization was quite literally built by tribes enslaving other tribes. That dynamic never disappeared, it abstracted into employment, law, and culture. Most of us were raised with industrial standards: become a specialist, get a high paying job. You understood the one skill to do your job, but didn't try to understand the system that paid you.

The problem is that slaves don't know they're slaves. Wage slavery is financial: if you cannot stop showing up to work without catastrophe, and you have no skills to create alternatives, you meet the definition of a slave, no matter what your feelings tell you.

## II – The five ingredients of success

If you don't create a routine, you will be assigned one. Five ingredients to becoming future-proof:

- **Agency** — the ability to "just do things" without permission.
- **Taste** — the experience to know what's worth putting out into the world.
- **Persuasion** — the skill to get people to care about what you do (not manipulation).
- **Persistence** — the understanding that mistakes do not equal death.
- **Iteration** — error-correcting toward a goal based on feedback.

AI is really great at asset creation, but hit creation is not asset creation. Asset creation is a necessary but insufficient condition for hit creation.

> Anyone could make a video game last week like anyone could make a video game 5 years ago... You know how many mobile games get put out a year? Thousands. You know how many hits are made in a year? Zero to five. — Strauss Zelnick

You can build anything, but that doesn't mean (1) it's worth building (2) people will care and (3) you have the ability to iterate and persist according to feedback.

## III – The antidote to employment is becoming unemployable

Dan's own story: landed his first web design client for $300, a hand-coded site for a local mattress company. That's when it clicked — repeat, improve, iterate, and gain control over lifestyle and future. It formed a deep conviction to never accept a job again.

**1) Hurl yourself into an environment that forces growth.** The fastest way to change your life is to rip yourself out of your physical and digital environment overnight. Behavior change = identity change. You imitate — it's called learning. It becomes bad when your behavior isn't conducive to the life your core is calling for.

**2) Choose a vessel where feedback is as close to reality as possible.** The most dangerous lifestyle is one removed from continuous trial and error. The mark of an intelligent system is that it course-corrects according to feedback.

**3) Learn 1 of these 2 skills if you want to thrive in the future.**

> Code and media are permissionless leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich. — Naval

Media (content) is more important than code, in Dan's view. The value of content is subjective — you need to know what good looks like, which AI can't give you because you haven't started the process of trial and error. The value of code is relatively objective — it just needs to work, but there are more mobile apps than ever with decreasing downloads, because they lack distribution.

## IV – How to start - set aside 15 minutes to change your trajectory

The only question that matters: what is your life's work? Not a personal brand — a life's work.

**Step 1: Excavate your raw material.** What do you know too much about for it to be an accident? What problem did you solve for yourself that you assumed everyone else already figured out? What did you get in trouble for as a kid that was actually just early taste?

**Step 2: Name your contrarian spine.** Taste is not about knowing what is good, it's about knowing what is broken and being unable to look away. What piece of mainstream advice actively made your life worse? What do you believe that experts would call naive, but you cannot shake?

**Step 3: Post your first idea tomorrow.** Take one answer from Step 1 and one from Step 2. Combine them into a single sentence only you could write. Publish it tomorrow. If it doesn't work, that's feedback — study, iterate, and try again until you master the skill.
